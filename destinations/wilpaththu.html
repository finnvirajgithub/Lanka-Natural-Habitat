Wilpattu

Wilpattu National Park

Located on the northwest coast of Sri Lanka, Wilpattu National Park is the country's largest and one of its oldest national parks. Spanning approximately 1,317 square kilometers, the park is renowned for its unique landscape, characterized by "willus" (natural sand-rimmed water basins), which fill up during the rainy season and serve as vital water sources for wildlife.

is famous for its elusive leopards, offering a high chance of spotting these majestic animals. It is also home to elephants, sloth bears, spotted deer, and various bird species, making it a paradise for wildlife enthusiasts and bird watchers. The park is lush with dense forests, open grasslands, and beautiful lakes, creating a scenic and serene environment that attracts travelers from around the world.

Key Highlights:

  • Location: Northwest Sri Lanka, around 30 km west of Anuradhapura.
  • Famous for: Leopard sightings, rich biodiversity, and scenic lakes.
  • Best time to visit: February to October (dry season).
  • Popular activities: Wildlife safaris, bird watching, and nature photography.

Wilpattu is an ideal destination for visitors who want to experience Sri Lanka’s wilderness, as it offers an immersive and unspoiled nature experience away from the crowds.
