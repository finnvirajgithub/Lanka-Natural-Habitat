Colombo City Tour

Colombo City Tour Itinerary

Stop 1: Gangaramaya Temple

Visit one of Colombo’s most iconic Buddhist temples, the Gangaramaya Temple. Known for its stunning architecture, which blends Sri Lankan, Thai, Indian, and Chinese styles, this sacred site offers insight into the local religious practices and culture.

Stop 2: Galle Face Green

Head to Galle Face Green, a popular oceanfront promenade where you can enjoy a relaxing stroll, take in the sea breeze, and watch locals flying kites. It’s an excellent spot for scenic photos with views of the Indian Ocean.

Stop 3: Colombo National Museum

Explore the Colombo National Museum, Sri Lanka’s largest museum, which houses an impressive collection of artifacts, paintings, sculptures, and ancient manuscripts that provide a deep dive into the country’s history and heritage.

Stop 4: Dutch Hospital

Visit the historic Dutch Hospital, a beautifully restored colonial building now home to high-end shops, cafes, and restaurants. Originally built in the 17th century, it offers a glimpse into Colombo’s colonial past while providing a modern dining and shopping experience.

Stop 5: Jami Ul-Alfar Mosque

Admire the striking red-and-white brickwork of the Jami Ul-Alfar Mosque, also known as the Red Mosque. This unique mosque, built in 1909, is one of Colombo’s most distinctive landmarks and is located in the busy Pettah market area.

Stop 6: Independence Square

End your tour at Independence Square, a historically significant site commemorating Sri Lanka’s independence from British rule in 1948. The Independence Memorial Hall is surrounded by lush greenery, making it a peaceful spot to reflect on the nation’s journey to freedom.
